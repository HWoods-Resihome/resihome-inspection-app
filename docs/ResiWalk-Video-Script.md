# ResiWalk Inspections — Team Training Video Script & Storyboard

**Purpose:** A narrated, in-depth walkthrough of the app: every major feature, then a full run from a Scope Rate Card → manager approval → QC Turn Re-Inspect of that same property.

**Runtime target:** ~9–11 minutes.

**How to produce it:** Record screen capture of the app following the *ON SCREEN* column, and lay the *VOICEOVER (VO)* lines over it. Tools that work well: Loom or QuickTime for screen capture + a mic; or paste the VO into an AI-voice tool (Descript, ElevenLabs, Synthesia) and drop your screen recording behind it. The VO is written to be read at a calm, confident pace.

**Voice & tone:** Friendly, expert, concise. Brand color is hot pink (#ff0060); the app is "ResiWalk."

---

## 0:00 — Cold open / title (0:00–0:20)

**ON SCREEN:** App logo / login screen, then a quick montage: a room with photos, the totals header, the AI review popup, a finalized PDF.

**VO:**
> "This is ResiWalk — the field app we use to scope a turn, capture the evidence, let AI check our work, and get it approved. In the next few minutes I'll walk you through everything it does, then take one real inspection from a Scope Rate Card all the way to a QC re-inspection. Let's go."

---

## 0:20 — Signing in & the home screen (0:20–1:10)

**ON SCREEN:** Sign in with Google. Land on the home list. Hover the search bar, then the status chips, then the two dropdown filters. Press-and-hold a card to show multi-select.

**VO:**
> "Sign in with your ResiHome email. You land on every inspection you can see. Search by address, name, or inspector. The chips up top filter by status — Scheduled, In Progress, Pending Approval, Completed — and the two dropdowns narrow by inspector or template.
>
> Tap any card to open it. And if you need to cancel a batch, just press and hold a card — that drops you into multi-select so you can clear several at once.
>
> One tip: install ResiWalk to your home screen. It gets an app icon and an offline shell, so it opens even when you've got no signal."

---

## 1:10 — Starting a Scope Rate Card (1:10–1:45)

**ON SCREEN:** Tap "+ New Inspection". Pick a property; show bed/bath. Open the template dropdown — pause on the grouped sections (Turn / PM / 1099 / QC). Choose "Scope Rate Card" under Turn. Pick today's date. The inspection opens; point at the status pill flipping to "In Progress" after the first edit.

**VO:**
> "To start, tap New Inspection, choose the property, and pick your template. Notice the templates are grouped — Turn, PM, ten-ninety-nine, and QC. We'll choose Scope Rate Card under Turn.
>
> Pick today to start now, or a future date to schedule it. The card opens to its rooms. As soon as you make your first edit, the status flips to In Progress automatically — no extra step."

---

## 1:45 — Line items: typing (1:45–2:45)

**ON SCREEN:** Open a room (e.g., Bedroom 1). Tap "+ Add Line Item". In the search box type "paint wall" — show "Paint 1 Wall" / "Paint 2 Wall" appearing (fuzzy match). Pick one. Set quantity, vendor, tenant %. Edit the description text. Tap Save. Then tap "+ Add Line Item" again immediately to show it opens a fresh row without a separate save.

**VO:**
> "Open a room and add a line. Search is fuzzy and ranked — type 'paint wall' and it surfaces Paint 1 Wall and Paint 2 Wall even though the words aren't adjacent. Pick your item.
>
> Set the quantity — the unit, each or square feet or linear feet, is shown so you measure the right thing. Choose the vendor and the tenant chargeback percent. You can fine-tune the description or set a custom vendor cost. The vendor, client, and tenant costs calculate live.
>
> Save it — and to keep going, just tap Add Line Item again. It finalizes the current row and opens a fresh one, so you can rip through a room."

**VO (overlay as you add a Whole House SF item and a paint item):**
> "Two smart defaults to know: in Whole House, a square-foot item auto-fills the property's square footage. And paint and flooring lines auto-set the tenant percent from our depreciation schedule based on how long the tenant was in the home — twelve months puts paint at seventy-five, flooring at ninety-five. Tub refinish is the exception; it stays at a hundred."

---

## 2:45 — Voice assistant (2:45–3:40)

**ON SCREEN:** Tap the mic in the footer. Speak: "Add two light bulbs in the kitchen and a drywall repair in the hallway." Show both lines landing in the right rooms. Then: "Full house sales clean, level one" — show the single Whole House clean line.

**VO:**
> "Now the fun part — voice. Tap the mic and just talk while you walk.
>
> Watch this: 'Add two light bulbs in the kitchen and a drywall repair in the hallway.' It splits that into two lines and files each in the right room — no menu-hunting.
>
> It's domain-aware. 'Full house sales clean, level one' adds the single Whole House clean line — it won't scatter little cleaning items across rooms. 'Mist match' is recognized as paint. And when a line needs a real measurement, like carpet square footage, it asks instead of guessing. Only mention a vendor or a tenant percent if it's different from the defaults."

---

## 3:40 — Photos & video (3:40–4:30)

**ON SCREEN:** Tap "Take". Snap a photo. Press-and-hold for a short video. Tap the Gallery button. Mark up a photo with an arrow. Tag a photo to a line item. Show the lightning-badge phone-camera button.

**VO:**
> "Evidence. Tap Take to open the camera. Snap a photo, or press and hold for a video clip — capped at twenty seconds so files stay manageable. The Gallery button pulls in existing photos, and the camera button with the lightning badge opens your phone's camera for a flash shot.
>
> You can mark up a photo — arrow, circle, pen — and tag a photo to a specific line item so the evidence travels with that line. If you're offline, photos compress and queue right on the device and upload the moment you're back on signal."

---

## 4:30 — Totals & breakdown (4:30–5:00)

**ON SCREEN:** Point to the header totals. Tap to expand the by-category breakdown. Tap "Expand all". Drill into a category's line items.

**VO:**
> "Your running numbers live in the header — lines, vendor, client, tenant, and net turn. Tap the totals to expand a breakdown by category, then drill into the line items. Expand all opens every category at once, so you can sanity-check the whole scope in one view."

---

## 5:00 — Offline & syncing (5:00–5:35)

**ON SCREEN:** Toggle airplane mode (or simulate). Add a line. Show the offline banner with the pending count. Re-enable signal; show "Syncing…" then the banner clearing.

**VO:**
> "ResiWalk is built for the field, so everything works offline — the catalog is cached and your edits and photos queue locally. The banner shows what's pending. When signal returns, it syncs automatically and clears.
>
> If something genuinely can't sync, the banner turns red with Retry, a Details button that shows exactly why, and a Clear option. And here's the safety net: you can't submit until everything has truly synced — so a manager never gets a half-saved scope."

---

## 5:35 — AI Scope Review (5:35–7:10) — the centerpiece

**ON SCREEN:** Tap the spark (AI Review) icon in the footer — show the amber badge. Suggestions stream into the popup: a tenant-% edit, a duplicate removal, a wrong-room move with the room dropdown, and a photo-gap item with Add photo / Remove / Ignore. Approve a couple, decline one, override a tenant % in the field. Tap Apply. Show the badge turn to a green check.

**VO:**
> "Before we submit, we run the AI review — and this is the heart of the app. It checks the scope against our standard: safe, clean, functional — no luxury upgrades — plus the depreciation and tenant-responsibility rules.
>
> Tap the spark icon. The badge tells you the status — amber means a review is needed, a green check means you're good for the current scope. Suggestions stream in one at a time, so the list stays put while you work.
>
> Look at what it catches: it'll right-size a tenant percent for depreciation; flag a duplicate line for removal; notice a line filed in the wrong room and let you move it with a dropdown; and when a damage line has no photo backing it up, it gives you Add photo — which opens the camera and tags the line — or Remove, or Ignore.
>
> For each one you Approve or Decline, and you can override the suggested tenant percent or quantity right here. It also knows the company rules cold — gutter cleaning and yard work are tenant; a whole-house paint makes the per-room paint lines redundant; carpet at one square foot gets flagged as a bad measurement.
>
> When you're happy, tap Apply. The app makes the changes, saves them, and marks the review complete. From here on, any change to the scope asks for a fresh review — but reassigning a vendor or editing photos won't; the review stays green."

---

## 7:10 — Submit for approval (7:10–7:35)

**ON SCREEN:** Tap "Submit for Approval". Show the status move to Pending Approval.

**VO:**
> "With required photos in, the scope synced, and the review complete, tap Submit for Approval. The inspection moves to Pending Approval and lands with the manager."

---

## 7:35 — Manager approval & finalize (7:35–8:30)

**ON SCREEN:** Switch to the approver's view (a Pending Approval inspection). Show they can run the AI review too. Tap "Finalize & Generate PDFs". Show the downloads modal: Master Report, Tenant Chargeback PDF, Vendor PDFs, then the xlsx last. Mention the email.

**VO:**
> "Now the manager. They open the pending inspection, review the scope and the photos, and they can run the AI review themselves — here it's a helper, not a gate; they can finalize whenever they're ready.
>
> When they finalize, ResiWalk generates the whole package — the Master Report, the Tenant Chargeback PDF, a PDF for each vendor, and the Tenant Chargeback import file in Excel, listed last — and emails it out. A built-in lock makes sure two people can't finalize the same one at once and create duplicates."

---

## 8:30 — QC Turn Re-Inspect, same inspection (8:30–9:40)

**ON SCREEN:** "+ New Inspection" → Turn → "Turn Re-Inspect QC". Pick the property, then select the *source Scope Rate Card* (the one just approved). Show its lines copy in. Walk through marking lines Pass/Fail with before/after photos. Finalize; show the verdict.

**VO:**
> "Last piece — closing the loop. After the turn work is done, QC validates it against the exact scope we approved.
>
> Start a new inspection, and under Turn choose Turn Re-Inspect QC. Pick the property, then choose the source Scope Rate Card — the one we just approved. Every line we paid a vendor for copies right in.
>
> Now QC walks the home and marks each line Pass or Fail, with before-and-after photos as proof the work was actually done. Finalize it, and you've got the verdict — pass and fail counts — and a QC report.
>
> That's the full loop: Scope Rate Card, manager approval, and a QC re-inspection of that same property — every dollar of scope verified on site."

---

## 9:40 — Close (9:40–10:00)

**ON SCREEN:** Back to the home list showing the inspection now Completed; subtle logo.

**VO:**
> "That's ResiWalk. Scope it fast — by hand or by voice — back it with photos, let the AI catch what we'd miss, and move it cleanly from the field to approval to QC. Get out there and turn some homes."
